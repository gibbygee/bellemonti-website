For twenty-five years, agile turned PM into a meeting management role. Good timing, really. Just as AI made judgment the only thing that matters.

## The PM problem

[Agile Has Broken Your Company](https://x.com/chamath/status/2028650332595896555?s=12) — Yes, Chamath is selling a product. I'm here for the argument anyway. Agile turned "responding to change" into an excuse to never finish anything. "Velocity" replaced quality — story points, burn-down charts, throughput, none of which measure whether the software is any good. The result: technical debt consuming 20–40% of engineering capacity, a 2023 GitLab survey finding only 12% of developers felt their codebase was well-documented, and the Standish Group reporting only 31% of software projects were considered successful in 2020. The Product Owner became a bureaucratic proxy — a layer between engineers and outcomes, distorting requirements at every handoff. PMs didn't just participate in this demise. We built the process that enabled it.

[The most underrated hire right now](https://x.com/signulll/status/2030404483897815089?s=12) — signüll makes the case for a new role: not a PM, but a "product thinker." Someone with an intuitive grasp of where the product is soft, where it sings, and how to move it. The argument: building is no longer hard. Engineering is no longer the bottleneck. Variance in outcomes has shifted almost entirely to judgment — what to build, how to sequence it, how to tell the story.

> *"The story matters as much as the thing. Internally, it organizes the team around a shared model of why. Externally, it shapes the interpretive frame users bring to their first experience. You can't retrofit narrative onto a product and expect it to land — it has to be load bearing from the start."*
>
> [signüll](https://x.com/signulll/status/2030404483897815089?s=12)

[Figma CEO Dylan Field on taste](https://x.com/r0ck3t23/status/2027333669828866477) — Field: "If an agent can do it for you, an agent can do it for someone else." When execution becomes free, execution becomes worthless. What remains is taste. The one thing an agent can't generate. The new job isn't building — it's selecting. Sample the widest possible space. Then be harsh enough to reject everything until something's worth keeping. We're all saying the same thing.

[Max Levchin on the jobs argument](https://x.com/mlevchin/status/2027473747633181151) — "AI means fewer software jobs is totally backwards." Most companies in the S&P500 would build their own software if they had the talent. AI means great engineers can be great engineers and great PMs can be great PMs. Those lousy at either can do something else.

## Services Are the New Software

[Services: The New Software](https://sequoiacap.com/article/services-the-new-software/) — Julien Bek at Sequoia. A copilot sells you the tool. An autopilot sells you the outcome. For every dollar spent on software, six are spent on services — and autopilots go after that six. The outsourced, intelligence-heavy task is the wedge. Go read the market map: insurance brokerage, accounting, healthcare revenue cycle, claims adjusting, tax advisory, legal, IT managed services, supply chain, recruitment, consulting. Every one of them is sitting on a pile of intelligence work that nobody wants to do.

The next generation of breakout companies will not be built in the same way or the same form as the last generation of breakout companies. Get over it.
