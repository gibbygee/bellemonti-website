Reminds me of that old 30 Rock gag... 

> We might not be the best people, but we're not the worst - graduate students are the worst!

PM as a profession has gotten very techno-logical. Very compartmentalized. The problem with this is that it conflicts - axiomatically - with the goal of being a PM. Which is to understand and interpret market signals.

The sad part about modern PMming is that it's gotten totally lost inside the "agile" dev cycle. The process has ate up a lot of PMs. The work became 

- WRITE THE TICKETS 
- LET'S GO 
- HURRY UP 
- WE. NEED. MORE. TICKETS.

When confronted with the inevitable question - "ok so why did you choose to do this?" the obvious answer becaome... well, we talked to sales/customers/support. 

They become, in essence, HIGHLY RESPONSIVE PMs. Which, I've concluded after witnessing this phenomenon so many times is just about as bad as not talking to anyone. 

When you have a savvy sales org (or certain customers) they figure this out right away. They start salmoning their way upstream to offer their OBVIOUSLY brilliant ideas. To be sure, this is what you pay sales reps to do - work the system. PMs are outgunned.

PMs have now become "waiters." They start creating tickets on behalf of those who are ordering. Often to the approval of their managers - LOOK HOW PRODUCTIVE I AM!! I CREATED ALL THE TICKETS!

Workin' for tips.

Scrutiny goes out the window, judgement goes out the window. Strategy - ie the series of well-considered choices made to advance the success of the product and the company - goes out the window.

It's a symptom I watch for now. It means the PM team is in a deep hole.
